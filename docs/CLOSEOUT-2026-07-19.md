## What got falsified

Four confident claims died today. Each was **true-but-insufficient** rather than false,
and each died to returning to raw data instead of accepting a summary.

1. **`params_all_on_cuda=True` meant a clean fit.** It didn't. Windows WDDM silently
   spills to shared system RAM instead of raising OOM. Placement was correct; peak VRAM
   exceeded physical. The CLEAN condition was restated: *peak must stay under physical*,
   not merely *params on cuda*.
2. **"217 WHEA events since May → pre-existing, unrelated."** True count, wrong
   inference. Bucketing by severity showed 213 corrected and 4 fatal, with three of the
   fatals occurring today during spike runs.
3. **Spill caused the crashes.** Refuted by deliberate control: 5 minutes of continuous
   fp16 spilling at 248 aa produced zero events and no crash. Then int8 — which does not
   spill — crashed at 630 aa anyway. Eliminated twice, independently.
4. **Corrected errors precede fatal ones.** *Inverted, not merely wrong.* In all four
   crashes, Event 1 logs in the **same second** as its corrected errors — they are crash
   debris, not a precursor ramp. And six historical burst days with 65/40/31 corrected
   errors produced **zero** fatals, while 5/27's three corrected errors accompanied one.
   Anti-correlated. Every rate-based comparison in S-002 was restated at source.

Also eliminated as crash explanations: **driver** (596.72 update — crash reproduced),
**LM Studio** (autostart disabled — crash reproduced), **single-fold duration**
(fp16 control survived 5 × 74 s folds; S-004 died at ~56 s).

## Method notes (added to the log preamble, governing future entries)

- **State a check precisely enough that its inadequacy is discoverable.** Three times
  today a check either fired or was shown insufficient. Vague work absorbs scrutiny
  without changing.
- **The rule extends to tool output, not just data.** "The harness reported a rejection"
  was accepted and repeated as fact; it was an artifact of an interrupt landing on an
  in-flight call. Same failure mode as the WHEA counts.
- **Verify a leading indicator's events actually precede what they predict.**
- **Prefer the instrument-free comparison when one exists.** The strongest evidence today
  — 4 crashes in 4 HER2 attempts, 0 in ~94 sub-ceiling folds — depends on no instrument at all.
- **Pre-register before the number exists, or not at all.** One grid was correctly
  refused because the run had already completed. Pre-registration against a known result
  is theater and would have destroyed the practice's value.
- **Name the outcome that would overturn the favored hypothesis.** Reading 4 —
  "host crashes with no prior corrected errors → escalation is not gradual" — was
  pre-registered and fired. Without it, the result would have been logged as something
  weaker.

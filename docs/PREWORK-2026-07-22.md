## 4. Test surface for the manifest — written before the code

"NOTCH2 1652, PTPRZ1 1612, LRP6 1351, JAG1 1034," taken from D-022's prose. Checked against
`data/cohort_82_ecd.csv`, **that is wrong** — all four are ≥ 1034 aa and sit in the `rental`

**The actual unrouted band is 13 targets in (440, 630) aa:**

    ADAM17 457 · ENTPD1 441 · SCNN1A 456 · MERTK 485 · CSF1R 498
    PDGFRB 500 · LRFN4 502 · LRFN3 523 · EPHA4 528 · GRIN1 541
    CDH11 564 · LRRN1 606 · EGFR 621

This materially raises the A6000 probe's stakes. It does not route four peripheral targets —
it routes **thirteen**, including **EGFR**, one of the most established ADC targets in the
cohort. A probe that was a tidy-up is now on the critical path for 16% of the cohort.

Tests to exist before the implementation:

- **Partition invariant.** Every one of the 82 accessions lands in exactly one bucket, and
  the bucket counts sum to 82. This is the test that makes the coverage line trustworthy
  rather than decorative. Assert the *measured* distribution: 40 / 16 / 13 / 13.
- **Named exclusions are present, not absent.** MUC16 (`Q8WXI7`) and FAT2 (`Q9NYQ8`) appear
  as **excluded rows with a stated reason** — D-022's whole point. A test asserting they are
  *missing* would encode the very bug the entry exists to prevent.
- **Boundary method per target** ∈ `{sliced_ecd, gpi_predicted, whole}` and every target has
  one. Per D-023 (ii), the GPI subset (MSLN `Q13421`, GPC1 `P35052`) routes to **`whole`,
  held out** until the predictor lands — *not* to `gpi_predicted`. Pin this, because the
  natural implementation reads D-021 and routes them to a method that does not yet exist.
- **`unrouted` is representable and correctly populated.** The 13 targets above are unrouted
  pending the ceiling — not assumed local, not assumed rental. Assert the exact set.
- **A red-on-change test for the A6000 ceiling**, in the D-009 §1 manner: when the config
  stops saying `UNMEASURED, conservative`, the test asserting "these 13 are unrouted" goes
  **red**, forcing the routing to be revisited rather than silently outlived.
- **The ceiling config carries its label in the value** (D-023 iii), not in a comment. A test
  reads the config and asserts the label is present while unmeasured.
- **SDK1 (`Q7Z5N4`) gets a test naming it specifically** — whatever D-024 rules. Its
  `None-2009(None)` span must not be parsed as a numeric boundary, and must not be silently
  bucketed with the 12 no-topology targets.
- **The 3 primary-match accessions** (ATP2B2, LRRN1, SMO) carry their mapping-provenance flag
  into the manifest, per D-020.

## 4a. CORRECTION — there are TWO ceilings, and the 13 belong to the local one

Raised by Code, confirmed at the artefact. §4's claim that the A6000 probe "routes thirteen,
including EGFR" is **wrong**, and it is the same failure mode §0 records: reasoning from an
entry's framing instead of the code.

- **`scripts/ecd_lengths.py:46-52`** — `CEILING_KNOWN_GOOD = 440` / `CEILING_KNOWN_BAD = 630`
  are commented *"Measured local ceiling, S-004/S-005"*, with the exact ceiling in (440, 630)
  explicitly UNMEASURED, which is why it buckets against both bounds. So `untested` means
  **unmeasured against the LOCAL ceiling.**
- **`worker/ceiling_probe.py`** docstring — measures the **A6000** ceiling to route the
  **rental** bucket's borderline: NOTCH2 1652, PTPRZ1 1612, LRP6 1351, JAG1 1034.

Two ceilings, therefore two borderline sets. The Planner read "borderline" as one global
category. D-022's prose was accurate about its own subject all along.

**Consequence for the tests:** §4's red-on-change test was wired to the A6000 config, which
would never move the 13. Under the D-024 ruling the 13 now route to rental outright, so the
trigger question is moot for coverage — but the *local* bisection remains open and cheap, and
moves targets from `tier_reason=unmeasured_local_ceiling` to a measured `local`. That is a cost
reduction, not a correctness fix. Recorded, not scheduled.

**The A6000 probe's stakes are genuinely raised — for the four large rental targets**, which is
what D-022 said. Not for EGFR.
